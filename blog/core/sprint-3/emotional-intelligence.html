Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence?
Emotional intelligence is the ability to recognise, understand, manage and express your emotions in a healthy manner.
It is the recognition, understanding and influencing the emotions of others. People with a high EQ make great leader and team players because they are able to empathise and connect with those around them.

How is it different to IQ?
Both EQ and IQ can impact your relationships, professional life and your wellbeing. Where EQ focuses on recognition and management of emotions in yourself and others.
IQ is your intelligence level, your ability to understand and apply information, logical reasoning, word comprehension and maths skills.
IQ is an inborn ability whereas EQ is a learned and improved ability.

Why is it important?
"If you do not manage your emotions, your emotions will manage you.” - Deborah Rozman (Psychologist and Author).
Your emotions precede thought, so when your emotions are in a heightened state they change the way your brain functions affecting the decisions you make and communication skills.
By recognising, understanding and managing your emotions we can be more successful in both your professional and personal life.

EQ is important in a both a personal and professional capacity, you are better able to have those uncomfortable conversation without offending or hurting feelings, managing your emotions when in a state of stress or feeling overwhelmed, most importantly it can help build and improve your relationships.
In a professional sense EQ is super important when it comes to managing conflict, motivating others and creating a collaborative environment.
